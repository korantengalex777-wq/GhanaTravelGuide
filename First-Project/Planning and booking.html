Astonishing Tourist Attraction sites in Ghana

HOLIDAY DESTINATIONS

1.ABURI BOTANICAL GARDENS

Visiting Information

The gardens are open daily from morning to evening. Entry fees are low and affordable. The best time to visit is during the dry season or early mornings. Visitors are encouraged not to pluck plants or litter the environment. The cool climate makes walking enjoyable.

Tourist Site Contacts

You can contact them via phone +233 244 074 040, +233 505 234 604, or +233 342 197 473, with email aburibotanicgardens@gmail.com, and find them on Instagram (@aburibotanicalgarden) or Facebook (@Aburi-Botanical-Gardens) for bookings and inquiries.

2.Cape Coast Castle

Visiting Information

Cape Coast Castle is open to visitors throughout the week. Visiting hours usually begin in the morning and close in the late afternoon. Guided tours are strongly recommended because they provide historical explanations of the slave dungeons, the Door of No Return, and colonial administrative rooms. Entry fees differ for locals and international visitors, with additional charges for guided tours and photography. The best time to visit is during the dry season when the weather is comfortable for walking around the castle. Visitors are expected to maintain silence and respect, especially in sensitive areas like the dungeons. Photography may be restricted in certain sections, and touching historical artifacts is not allowed. Comfortable footwear is advised due to uneven floors and staircases.

Tourist Site Contacts

You can contact Cape Coast Castle through the Ghana Museums and Monuments Board (GMMB) via phone at +233-3321-32529 or 03321- 32701, or try their general email, ingo@momaa.org, though it's best to use the GMMB contact for official inquiries.

3.LARABANGA MOSQUE

Visiting Information

The mosque is open to visitors daily, but visits must not disrupt prayer times. Entry fees are minimal and mainly used for maintenance. The best time to visit is during daylight hours. Visitors must dress modestly and remove shoes before entering. Photography may require permission.

Tourist Site Contacts

The Larabanga Mosque, Ghana's oldest mosque, doesn't have a readily available direct contact number online, but you can visit the place directly.

4.BOTI WATERFALLS

Visiting Information

Boti Waterfalls is open daily to visitors. Entry fees are modest and vary by visitor category. The best time to visit is during the rainy season when the waterfalls are most powerful. Visitors must climb steps to access the falls, so moderate fitness is required. Safety rules include staying within designated paths and avoiding slippery rocks.

Tourist Site Contacts

To contact Boti Falls, you can reach out to the official management via email at admin@botifallsghana.com or call +233 54 692 9279 / +233 54 412 3030 / +233 55 303 0517 with work hours generally 7am-7pm weekdays and 10am-5pm weekends.

5.LEGON BOTANICAL GARDENS

Visiting Information

The gardens are open daily and operate mostly during daylight hours. Entry fees are affordable, with extra charges for recreational activities such as playground use and adventure rides. The best time to visit is during weekends or public holidays when activities are fully operational. Visitors are encouraged to follow safety instructions during activities and keep the environment clean.

Tourist Site Contacts

You can contact the Legon Botanical Gardens using phone numbers 0262 366 167, 0552 880 908 or via email at mulchcompanyltd@gmail.com or mulchcompanyltd@gmail.com. They are located off the Atomic Haatso Main Road at Agbogba Junction in Accra, and their digital address is GA- 575-2433.

6.KWAME NKRUMAH MEMORIAL PARK AND MAUSOLEUM

Visiting Information

The memorial park is open daily and welcomes visitors from morning to evening. Entry fees are affordable and vary between local and foreign visitors. The best time to visit is in the morning or late afternoon to avoid the midday heat. Visitors are expected to dress modestly and behave respectfully, especially near the mausoleum where Dr. Kwame Nkrumah and his wife are buried. Eating, loud conversations, and running are discouraged within the park.

Tourist Site Contacts

You can contact the Kwame Nkrumah Memorial Park & Mausoleum via their official email info@knmp.gov.gh, phone numbers like +233 53 007 6746 or +233 50 449 3993, visit their website knmp.gov.gh.

7.KAKUM NATIONAL PARK

Visiting Information

Kakum National Park operates daily, including weekends and public holidays. Visitors must register at the entrance before entry. The canopy walkway is the main attraction and requires visitors to follow strict safety rules. Entry fees vary for locals and foreigners, and additional fees may apply for guided tours. The best time to visit is early morning when wildlife activity is higher and the weather is cooler. Visitors are advised to wear comfortable clothing and shoes, and children must meet height and age requirements for the canopy walk.

Tourist Site Contacts

You can contact Kakum National Park via phone numbers like 0501291687, 0595533033, or 0200420831 for general inquiries or special arrangements, or use their official website kakumpark.com .
